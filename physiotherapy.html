Physiotherapy

Home › Physiotherapy
Physiotherapy takes a "whole person" approach to health and well-being. Our physiotherapists are trained to assess, treat, and empower you to a pain-free life by enabling you to manage your problems or injuries. Physical rehabilitation involves exercise therapy, postural correction, gait and balance re-training, manipulation and mobilisation of tissues and joints.

Our Approach

What We Treat

The physiotherapy service at Shanthi Wellness offers a complete range of consultation, examination, and treatment services. Our licensed physiotherapists assess the need for orthotics and recommend adaptive equipment tailored to each patient's condition.

We help encourage development and facilitate recovery, enabling people to stay in work while helping them remain independent for as long as possible.

🤲 Manual Therapy

A specialised form of physical therapy delivered with hands. Practitioners use their hands to put pressure on muscle tissue and manipulate joints in an attempt to decrease pain caused by muscle spasm, muscle tension, and joint dysfunction.

🦴 Chiropractic Treatment

Used most often to treat neuromusculoskeletal complaints including back pain, neck pain, pain in the joints of arms or legs, and headaches. Uses spinal manipulation to restore joint mobility by manually applying a controlled force into restricted joints.

💊 Pain Management

Important for ongoing pain control, especially for long-term or chronic pain. After a thorough pain assessment, our doctors prescribe appropriate pain medicine, treatments, or psychotherapy for pain relief.

  • Neck Pain
  • Headaches
  • Joint Pain
  • Sports Injuries
  • Acute & Chronic Pain
  • Biomechanical Dysfunction

👶 Pediatric Conditions

Physical therapy helps develop the strength and range of motion children need to move through their environment easily. Goals include help with developmental milestones like sitting, standing, crawling and walking.

  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Down Syndrome
  • Erb's Palsy
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Muscular Dystrophy
  • Idiopathic Scoliosis
  • Hydrocephalus
  • Juvenile Rheumatoid Arthritis
  • Childhood Obesity
  • Congenital Torticollis
  • Hereditary Paraplegia
  • Klumpke's Paralysis

🧠 Neurological Conditions

Neurological diseases are disorders of the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. A treatment plan typically involves physiotherapy to align and activate joints and muscles to enable better postural control and stability.

  • Stroke
  • Scoliosis
  • Disc Prolapse
  • Plantar Fasciitis
  • Spinal Cord Injury
  • Multiple Sclerosis
  • Bell's Palsy
  • Parkinson's Disease
  • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome
  • Low Back & Neck Pain
  • Hip & Knee Pain
  • Nerve Impingement

🦵 Orthopaedic Conditions

Orthopaedic injuries affect the muscle, joint and nervous system. Techniques include strength training, hot/cold packs, joint mobilisations and manipulations, and electrical stimulation.

  • Sprains
  • Sciatica
  • Spondylosis
  • ACL Injuries
  • Tennis Elbow
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Frozen Shoulder
  • Sports Injuries
  • Rotator Cuff Tendinitis
