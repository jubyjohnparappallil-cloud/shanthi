Ancient Science of Life

Ayurveda — The Knowledge of Life

A 5,000-year-old holistic healing system that restores balance of body, mind and spirit through personalised care.

Home›Ayurveda
Ayurveda is the eternal Vedic science of life — the traditional natural healing system of India. Today, Ayurveda is at the forefront of mind-body medicine. Its basic principles are believed to have been remembered by Brahma, the Creator himself, and handed down through the great seers. Ayurveda literally means knowledge of life — a science that attempts to set out good and bad practices of life, outlining the cause of happy and unhappy life, what is wholesome and unwholesome.

Our Philosophy

Healing Through Balance

Ayurvedic medicines are based on the principle that every individual has a unique constitution related to vital energies — translated in Ayurvedic nosology as dosha or humors. The equilibrium of these humors depends on a variety of factors.

The objective of Ayurveda is to reestablish the equilibrium when we are ill and to maintain that equilibrium in order to keep us healthy. Ayurveda explores how to heal our minds on all levels — from the subconscious to super-conscious — along with the role of diet, exercise and many other methods to create wholeness.

Ayurveda mainly comprises of eight clinical specialties: General Medicine, Pediatrics, Psychiatry, Treatment of Head & Neck, Surgery, Toxicology, Rejuvenation Therapy and Reproductive Medicine.

The Essence of Ayurveda

Panchakarma — Five-Fold Purification

Panchakarma is the essence of Ayurvedic treatment. It became popular due to the magical results it can bring out even on otherwise incurable diseases. Purifying the body and regaining the normal rhythm through a healthy line of treatment is a rare concept of medication — and that is the main strength of Panchakarma.

The five operative procedures are: Emesis (Vamana), Purgation (Virechana), Oil Enema (Anuvasana), Decoction Enema (Niruha) and Errhines (Nasya).

Kerala has modulated many special treatments including Pizhichil, Navara Kizhi and Shirodhara — major tools in rejuvenation therapy and providing relaxation to the stressed and strained.
